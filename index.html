On the 63-th day of the protest the Yanukovych government started killing Ukrainians, mostly with firearms.

On January 22 Ukraine celebrates the Unity Day, a celebration of consolidating different historical regions into one state. In the early hours of the festive day three unarmed protesters got fatal gunshot wounds - Serhiy NIHOYAN from Dnipropetrovsk oblast, Mykhaylo ZHYZNEVSKYY (a Belarusian emigrant) from Kyiv oblast and Roman SENYK from Halychyna.

The mass murder on the Kyiv streets would break out in 27 days.

This is a story about the people who were killed during the protests in the centre of Kyiv during February 18-20, 2014. No matter on which side of the barricades they parted with life.

It should be emphasized that our material is neither an investigation nor an attempt to deliver a judgment before the completion of the judicial proceedings.

First we should arrange all available information about the circumstances of death of those people: who, when, where, and how got a fatal wound?

To do this, we turned to three groups of sources:

  • - conclusions of the investigation team of the General Prosecutor’s Office,
  • - documentary videos,
  • - online reports.

But the DATA available as of the beginning of March 2017 DO NOT PROVIDE THE ENTIRE COMPREHENSIVE information. So we had to make assumptions, especially regarding the deaths on February 18-19. This task is still very far from completion.

Special Investigation Department of the General Prosecutor’s Office of Ukraine will need another several months, if not years, to find out all the details of the tragic events that occurred during the final days of mass protests. We will also assist in search, updating this project with every fact found. We would appreciate any help in establishing the truth.

І. February 18. Demonstration and clashes in the government quarter

The day before the cessation of actions was established. The protesters leave the premises of Kyiv City Council and partially deblock Hrushevskoho street, opening a way through the rows of barricades. Until February 18 all large-scale violence episodes during the street protests in Kyiv occurred on these barricades.

The political opposition hopes that a part of the Party of Regions faction would support the idea to limit the power of Yanukovych by reinstatement of the Constitution of 2004.

For the pressure on Parliament in the morning of February 18, 2014 the “peaceful offensive” goes to the Verkhovna Rada.

The march participants are far from being in peaceful mood - three months have passed since the beginning of the protests, and the government still has not responded to any demand of Maidan.

The events of that day are still the least investigated. This is especially true concerning the geography of violence in the government quarter – the data available today contain no specific addresses, but only the “Mariyinskyi park” or “Instytutska street.”

In such cases we have determined the APPROXIMATE PLACE of getting fatal wounds with appropriate comments in the description. We based our research on the general chronology of clashes, time of death (finding of a body) and nature of injuries.

Event times are approximate.

PROTESTERS GO TO VERKHOVNA RADA. The “Berkut” gets combat ammunition

08:00

At Maidan columns of demonstrators are formed. They are defended by the fighters of the Self-Defense (“Samooborona”) having protective gear (helmets, body armor, shields) and sticks to withstand a possible attack of security forces.

At the same time at Parkova drive since 8 a.m. the Antimaidan mass meeting participants get off the buses. The people brought here are mostly the public sector employees, but there are also some strong athletes.

Verkhovna Rada and a part of the Mariyinskyi park is surrounded by four lines of Internal Troops and the “Berkut”.

The day before some “Berkut” soldiers get combat ammunition instead of non-letal ones. This fact is proved by the court testimony by a soldier of Kharkiv “Berkut” Squadron, Shapovalov. The same kind of ammunition will be provided again on February 18 in the evening near Zhovtnevyy Palalce.

The bullets with lead canister shot are given without restriction and not recorded in any documents. A police officer who organized the issuance of these bullets to the personnel deliberately aimed to kill people.

09:30

A column of protesters marches through Instytutska and Kriposnyy lane to Hrushevskoho street. They drive away the KamAZ trucks which were blocking the way. Clashes are minimal. Some protesters are trying to break through the security lines to Verkhovna Rada.

In the office of the Verkhovna Rada Speaker Oleksandr Rybak unsuccessful negotiations continue on voting concerning the law on the Constitution 2004.

10:10

At the intersection of Instytutska and Sadova street, the police with “Fort” guns started maiming people shooting traumatic ammunition and throwing concussion grenades directly into the crowd.

Demonstrators set on fire the KamAZ trucks blocking Sadova. Then some soldiers from Internal Troops surrender to their opponents. The Self-Defense protects them from the angry crowd. Clashes continue until lunchtime.

10:30

There are several thousands of protesters gathered in Mariyinskyi park. They surround Antimaidan from three sides, from Hrushevskoho to the Dnipro slopes, fulfilling a plan to block Verkhovna Rada -- until the "Regions" and the Communists join the voting for the Constitution-2004.

The most efficient Self-Defense hundreds are concentrated there. First they have been leading the march, and now they are patrolling the perimeter – going to the corners of the park farthest from Hrushevskoho.

“ENHANCED HUMANITARIAN AID”. Russian grenades for the “Berkut”

10:52

A column of security officials moved from Rada to Shovkovychna. They carried green boxes with some ammunition. Obviously, these were Russian "enhanced special means", significantly more powerful than their Ukrainian analogues.

Over two months of confrontation the “Berkut” used so many grenades that the MIA had to procure more from Russia, spending the total amount of UAH 1.2 million.

On the January 21, An-12 aircraft which departed from a military airfield near Moscow landed in the Zhulyany airport. According to the delivery note from the Russian Ministry of Internal Affairs it carried more than 6,000 units of special means: concussion grenades "Fakel-S", "Zarya-2" and “Plamya-M", aerosol and smoke grenades, irritating grenades "RGR" - ordinary and the “enhanced” ones.

But this was not enough, as MIA extensively used the new grenades during clashes on Hrushevskoho. On January 24 An-12 makes another flight to Moscow. The second batch of special means - more than 7,300 units - lands in Gostomel.

In accompanying documents these items were listed as the “humanitarian aid.”

New grenades are authorized for use by the police without any examination by the Ministry of Health, although their sound and effect is much more powerful than of those the soldiers of the “Berkut” used on November 30 and December 1.

The security officers further enhance the already enhanced effect of special means, taping improvised “fragmentation elements” - fastening nails, screws or other small metal objects - to the grenades.

SECURITY FORCES USE COMBAT BULLETS. Arson of the Party of Regions’ office

11:00

Clashes near Verkhovna Rada begin. Massive explosions of concussion grenades are evidenced.

According to the Head of the Special Investigation Department of the General Prosecutor’s Office of Ukraine Sergiy Horbatyuk, from 11 o'clock that day combat weapons were again used on the streets of Kyiv.

Only demonstrators got the first gunshot wounds. No security officers were found among those injured at that time.

While peaceful march continued, the “Berkut” soldiers were hiding in the yards. Some of them accessed the roofs. They targetedly fired their "Forts". They threw down concussion grenades directly amid the crowds of people.

11:20

The “Berkut” soldiers who tried to attack Shovkovychna were chased away with stones. The majority of people doing it were not the trained fighters of the Self-Defense, but mostly the ordinary “civilians”. However, they braced up a fierce resistance, even though a month ago they considered similar actions of “extremists” on Hrushevskoho a provocation.

One of Maidan leaders led some part of protesters from Instytutska to the central office of the Party of Regions (Lypska street, 10).

~11:25

Having broken the door, the protesters kicked the employees out of the office and seized the Party documents. Subsequently, these documents would help investigate the crimes of the regime. The ground floor and the basement of the house were set on fire from the “Molotov cocktail”.

Apparently, around that time the 65-year-old man from Kyiv, Volodymyr Zakharov. died in the fire. He worked as an IT support specialist in the building where the Party of Regions office was located. His daughter-in-law Victoria cited the official information from physicians: “The autopsy showed no signs of blows or violence, he just died of suffocation ...»

The investigation confirmed that the body of the deceased had no signs of violent death. Zakharov suffocated due to carbon monoxide concentration, staying for too long in the smoky room to save at least some hardware from the workplaces of the office workers.

Should this victim be among the Hundred of Heaven? Wikipedia includes him and the memorial photo of Zakharov can be found at the Instytutska. He remained in office when all others fled. He fell another victim of the grandiose and tragic events, and also deserves being remembered.

The death of Zakharov is connected with the violent events, but is not actually a homicide, unlike most of the deaths described here.

MARIYINSKYI PARK. Self-Defense against Antimaidan

Meanwhile, in the Mariyinskyi park Antimaidan hurls stones at the Self-Defense from behind the police line.

Here in the Antimaidan camp the battle groups are formed from the “Titushky” (mercenary thugs) (and possibly other supporters of Yanukovych). They obtain shields with the inscription "Police", Soviet-style iron helmets, sticks and pipes.

The “Titushky” equipped like the Self-Defense and acting in close coordination with the “Berkut” are a know-how of the regime.

Around noon real street battles break out. The Self-Defense manages to push Internal Troops back several dozens of meters into the Antimaidan camp. Internal troops leave behind them fence shields which protesters are trying to use for defense perimeter.

Members of Parliament from the Party of Regions start fleeing from the session room. The number of injured is growing rapidly. Stones, “Molotovs” and Russian concussion grenades “enhanced” with tape and nails are hurled at protesters. One of the commanders of the Self-Defense has his hand torn off with such a grenade.

Demonstrators organize a hospital for the wounded in the House of Officers (on the corner of Hrushevskoho and Kriposnyy lane).

During this time Antimaidan is preparing for a counterattack. To help the fighters distinguish “their” people from similarly-equipped Self-Defense they use the identification marks - white and red armbands. These colours can hardly have any ideological meaning, the tape is simply the cut pieces of plastic signal tape which police typically uses to enclose a crime scene. The “committed” fighters with fascism from the opposition also wear St.George’s Ribbon.

Yanukovych's supporters do not use the colors of the national flag of Ukraine at all, while the clothes of protesters is so richly decorated with them.

Hiding behind the lines of the Internal Troops, Antimaidan members wearing helmets and using sticks pluck stones out of the pedestrian pavements and bring them closer to a possible place of clashes. The “Supporters of current President" are divided into groups, managed by coordinators who tell them what to do.

From the stage of Antimaidan Soviet war songs are played very loudly all the time and calls are heard to destroy the monsters-Nazis.

13:00

Around 13:00 the members of “Oplot” Kharkiv organization get in the rear of protesters by swooping around Hrushevskoho from the Arsenal factory This organization later start fighting on the Russian side in Donbas. The Self-Defense counterattacks and chases away Oplot members. The "prisoners" have burst packets with nails fastened to them and traumatic weapons.

The situation in Mariyinka is inactive. An ambulance is called for the speaker of Verkhovna Rada Rybak allegedly because of heart problems. The opposition manages to register a draft of resolution concerning voting for the Constitution of 2004. However, no one is left to vote.

In the early 1.p.m. fifty soldiers of the “Berkut” attack from Orlyka street to get back the office. At the forefront the security officers with pump action guns are marching. They are followed by the Titushky with iron pipes.

ПAfter a brief confrontation the office of the Party of Regions gets under the control of the police. Protesters retreat to the intersection of Lypska and Instytutska.

13:20

Another attack on the corner of Shovkovychna and Instytutska. The “Berkut” goes in a parade fashion, firing their "Forts". Reporter Anastasia Bereza cannot find proper words to describe it. She repeats the most shocking detail twice: “They shoot right into the crowd.” Each shot is accurate. Those who fall or stay back are violently beaten with clubs.

Around the same time a co-owner of the corporation "Industrial Union of Donbass" Serhiy Taruta calls Oleksandr Yanukovych. The latter is the eldest son of the President - the leader of the Donetsk “Young team” Viktor Yanukovych trusts him the most, delegating him more and more authority since 2012.

Taruta asks his Donetsk counterpart to convince his father about the inadmissibility of the force option.”Perhaps [Maidan] is our destiny,” - answers Yanukovych Junior. “This is our test. So we have to go through. We have to serve the country, to save it from these idiots”

13:30

Protesters are pushed back to Instytutska, a part of them moving towards Maidan and a part toward Kriposnyy lane. Some more people are standing behind the corner of Shovkovychna. Reinforcement comes to the security forces from Verkhovna Rada.

13:43

The “Berkut” finally takes control over the intersection of Shovkovychna and Instytutska. They form the "testudo" shape structure from their shields, blocking the passage through Instytutska toward Lypska (to Maidan) and Kriposnyy Lane (to the Mariyinskyi park). After 10 minutes they also have a water cannon in place.

Instytutska street was the only communication way in the government quarter which allowed the protesters to maneuver freely. Capturing the key intersection, the security officers divided the participants of the morning demonstration into two separate groups. The Self-Defense fighters, people most prepared for street clashes, are cut off from the Maidan.

~14:00

About 14:00 there are clashes between the “Berkut” and the Self-Defense in the Kriposnyy lane. This small street connects possible escape routes for participants of the march - Klovsky descent and Hrushevskoho (towards Arsenal).

Around this time, a 57-year-old Volodymyr KISCHUK — an entrepreneur from Dymer (Kyiv region) stops responding by phone. His was killed with a shot in the head near Kriposnyy lane.

Then a 44-year-old security guard from Kyiv Serhiy Shapoval, a fighter of the 21th Hundred of Maidan Self-Defense also died. Near the Kriposnyy lane he was shot with hunting canister shot, two pieces of lead pierced his stomach and heart.

Around the same time as Shapoval dies Ihor SERDYUK (aged 44) — an entrepreneur from Kremenchuk. He was building a barricade at the intersection of Kriposnyy lane and Instytutska to delay the breakthrough of security forces. Serdyuk was shot in the face with hunting canister shot.

After resting for a while, “Titushky” organized into combat groups and covered by the Internal Troops, to the repeating song “Svyashchennaya Voyna” (“Sacred War”) attack the Self-Defense in Mariyinka.

The forces are obviously unequal, the perimeter is broken and narrowing. Then the Internal Troops get in the rear of the Self-Defense from Hrushevskoho, blocking the only way to retreat.

Internal Troops soldiers, mostly young regular soldiers, are using their clubs as enthusiastically as the “Berkut” did. Titushky move behind them and brutally batter fallen people with stones and pipes.

A fighter of the 3rd hundred Miroslav Gai later told the Texty newspaper in his interview that in those moments he clearly understood: an agreement with the government failed. Do you want to survive? Be ready to kill those who seek to kill you.

14:15

About 50 Self-Defense fighters left in Mariyinka were pressed to the long grey “stalinka” house on the edge of the park on Hrushevskoho. Some of them managed to get inside and hide in the apartments of people living there.

The less lucky were left to lie outside the house and on the brick paths of the park. Puddles of blood were clearly visible on the February snow. Those still conscious were taken to the camp of Antimaidan.

“Like two different poles, we are opposed in everything – a song is heard over the battlefield. - For light and peace we fight and they – for the kingdom of darkness!”

Probably then the 49-year-old Andriy Korchak, a construction worker from Boryslav (Lviv Region), got fatally wounded. A “dozen leader” (desyatnyk) of the 3rd Hundred of the Self-Defense was ruthlessly beaten and maimed in Mariyinskyi park.

The damage to the chest caused chronic ischemic heart disease, and Korchak died in hospital, where he was taken by an unidentified volunteer in his Lada car. At 15:20 the protester was dead.

14:30

The correspondent of "Ukrayinska Pravda" reports that one of the protesters under the “«Berkut»” fire started and drove "in an unknown direction", one of the police trucks that blocked Hrushevskoho at the corner of the Kriposnyy lane.

This protester was Leonid Bibik who just escaped from a violent clash in Mariyinka. With the police GAZ he planned to clear the escape routes for demonstrators through the barriers of the “Berkut” along Kriposnyy lane to Instytutska to join the other protesters.

Chasing away the bewildered security officers, Bibik drives along the lane to the intersection with Instytutska — only to see that the way to Maidan is already blocked by the “Berkut”. He somehow manages to turn and drive back to Kriposnyy lane when two police officers shoot him in the head with their nonlethal pistols at a point blank distance.

Losing consciousness, Bibik still presses the accelerator pedal and breaks to Hrushevskoho. At that moment a 44-year-old Serhiy Didych, a dozen leader of the Self-Defense and the Head of the local Svoboda party organization in one of the regions of Ivano-Frankivsk runs into the street and gets under the wheels of the car. Didych was beaten by the “Berkut” officers, so one of them was chasing him and nearly got under the wheels too.

The Self-Defense dozen leader died on the spot – from an open head injury.

After 1.5 years Bibik, who at that time served as ATO, was accused and immediately granted amnesty.

RAISING STAKES. Maidan shoots back

"The peaceful offensive" failed because “Titushky” and security officers in the government quarter got ready to kill.

At least some of them will not hesitate to press the trigger or smash the skull of another person with an iron pipe. This was not true for the intellectuals and people from rural regions who participated in Maidan Self-Defense, who attached on the barricades near the House of Trade Unions a poster with a rhetorical appeal to their opponents, "TO KILL AND THEN LIVE WITH IT?"

So the authorities again raised the stakes in the confrontation: “We are ready not only to kill and maim, but to do it systematically, by dozens. And you?”

The determination of authorities to use violence yielded impressive results. Subsequently, in a few hours Maidan lost its core defenders and all territorial achievements of the past 90 days, shrinking down to a small area around the stage.

In the afternoon on February 18, few outside observers would doubt that the protest would last for only several hours. “That's the end”, people wrote in social networks in despair.

And at the moment of greatest triumph of the armed and trained policemen over defenseless civilians Maidan accepts the price.

According to the investigators of the GPU, from about 2 p.m. that day, the gunshot wounds of officers of the Ministry of Internal Affairs were first documented.

So on the 90-th day of protest Maidan takes up arms, while some protesters were shot with firearms a month ago.

ІІ. February 18. Defeat

14:45

The corner of Sadova and Instytutska is still controlled by the demonstrators. They burn tires of "KamAZ" trucks and exchange stone throwing with the Internal Troops standing in line.

14:56

The first reports of dead protesters. Lesya Orobets: “Three dead bodies in the House of Officers. They are our people. 7 more people are dying. The ambulance vehicles are blocked !!!" Doctor Olga Bogomolets confirms the death of three protesters.

The "Party of Regions" invites Turchynov to negotiations with Yanukovych. The group of negotiators gets on foot to the Presidential Administration through the barricades, fire and smoke. On some blocked streets violent clashes continue.

After five minutes’ hysterical debate with swearing and threats Yanukovych agrees to a compromise: MIA stops shooting and maiming, and protesters return to Maidan Nezalezhnosti. Turchynov calls to the Self-Defense commanders reporting about another cessation of hostilities.

Two bodies in the House of Officers are Kischuk and Didych. The third isSerhiy Shapoval.

According to the General Prosecutor Office investigators, Kharkiv “Berkut” officer Oleksandr Belov was probably involved in the murder of Kischuk, Shapoval and Serdyuk. Traces of lead were found in the barrel of his “Fort”.

“FORT-500” is a smoothbore pump action shotgun produced in Ukraine, which was used by “Berkut”.

After the murders on January 22 the MIA always named this weapon the “specialized means to fire non-lethal ammunition.” This meant the rubber – in the MIA terminology "elastic" - bullets and canister shot (a large canister shot of 0.5-1 cm), placed in the 12-gauge cartridges.

MIA does not add that the 12 mm gauge is standard for smoothbore guns. Their cartridges may use many types of ammunition. For example, hunting ammunition with lead bullet or canister.

In November 2016 the Judge of the Kyiv Court of Appeal Valeriy Lashkevych /> released Belov from detention, changing a preventive measure to the “night home arrest” ”.

15:05

The demonstrators retreating from the government quarter through Hrushevskoho street are trying to build a barricade using buses near the Arsenalna metro station (opposite the polyclinic at 2 Mazepa street). The attempt is unsuccessful, as the “Berkut” pushed them to the Slava square.

15:10

“Titushky” violently attack the house at Hrushevskoho 9 (near the Mariyinskyi Park), where the surviving Self-Defense fighters are hiding. They search through staircases and attics, try to break into the apartments. The protesters that get caught are brutally beaten. The prisoners are bound and taken to Antimaidan camp.

But the 26-year-old Artem Mazur, a security guard from Khmelnytskyi and a fighter of the 15th Hundred of the Self-Defense could not see this, because he was in a coma after Yanukovych supporters of in Mariyinskyi Park broke his skull. He died in hospital in early March, the column "cause of death" in his medical record stated “open craniocerebral trauma”.

The Self-Defense was crushed. Those who survived and were not afraid tried to get to Maidan. The information about the cessation of hostilities only confused people.

«CLEANSING» OF INSTYTUTSKA. Elderly people are killed, the attackers break their skulls

~16:00

About 4 p.m. The “Berkut” backed up by water cannons and “Titushky” again attacked at the intersection Shovkovychna-Instytutska, this time from three sides. They drove people towards Maidan, pelting them with stones and “Molotov cocktails”.

The “Berkut” knocks demonstrators down with clubs and kicks, “Titushky” smash them with iron pipes.

Yanukovych is not answering Turchynov’s calls from Verkhovna Rada. However, Turchynov manages to reach the Head of Presidential Administration Andriy Klyuyev, who is considered one of the masterminds of the November 30 breakup.

One of the victims on Instytutska was a 82-year-old Ivan Nakonechny from Kyiv, the retired Navy officer, the oldest of the Hundred of Heaven. His skull was crushed by a “metal object”, and three weeks later he died in the hospital.

Another senior protester, a 72-year-old man from Kyiv Anatoliy Nechyporenko, who worked as a guard at garage cooperative, could not escape from Titushky.

He also fell into a coma after a blow to the head getting a closed craniocerebral trauma and a depressed fracture of the skull. Nechyporenko died in hospital in early April.

When he could not reach Yanukovych by phone, Turchynov called Klyuyev, and informed about the agreement with Yanukovych.

"THIS IS WAR – answers the Head of the Presidential Administration to Turchynov concerning his accusations about the attack of the security forces during the cessation of hostilities - And during war all means will do.”

LOSS OF BARRICADES. Crush at the Khreschatyk metro station

16:03

The Security Service of Ukraine and the Ministry of Internal Affairs put forward an ultimatum to the opposition - "stop the confrontation" [i.e. leave Maidan] until 18:00.

16:06

the “Berkut” attack a barricade on the top of Instytutska near Khreschatyk metro station. The doors of the upper station close.

16.07

... because according to the decision of the Head of Kyiv City State Administration the Kyiv metro stopped its operation for the first time over the 53 years of its existence. At all stations and in all trains passengers are asked to leave the metro.

16:10

Internal Troops quickly break through the barricades on Hrushevskoho and go to Yevropeyska Square. They dismantle some of the legendary barricades, expanding the way – for armored vehicles, as it turned out later.

Soon concrete blocks would be brought there blocking Hrushevskoho with ultra-strong barricades or a checkpoint.

Protesters retreat to the barricade at Khreschatyk, near the House of Trade Unions. The poster asking “To kill, and then live with it?" has already disappeared - it is clear that the government wants the extermination war rather than talking. Barricades are still decorated with another appeal: “Please understand: we ARE SICK OF IT!"

The Internal Troops soldiers at Hrushevskoho are guarding the concrete blocks they have mounted. They also write appeals on their barricade. But they were not appeals to protesters. They do not address the outside world at all.

The inscriptions are made in black marker on the BACK of the concrete fortification. They are addressed to somebody somewhere inside the perimeter.

“Give us GUNS, — ask the black letters. - ... A complete freedom of law enforcement”.

16:18

Protesters again occupy the building of the Kyiv City State Administration.

16:20

the “Berkut” takes the top barricade at Instytutska (near the Khreschatyk metro station). A terrible stampede occurs, people start suffocating.

A barricade built of sacks filled with snow had only one narrow passage, surrounded by barbed wire. This idea of rebel engineers almost led to a mass tragedy.

Escaping from the “Berkut” in panic, dozens of demonstrators poured into the narrow passage so intensely that they got squeezed. Compressed and trapped, pressed against each other, they could not move, and the “Berkut” walked on top of the heap of human bodies, mercilessly hitting them with sticks.

Some “Berkut” officers, on the contrary, were savinglives, pulling suffocating people from the heap of bodies and protecting them from their brutal colleagues. If not for the humanism of these officers, there would be much more victims.

When the human heap dissolved, the bodies of a man and a woman were left lying the barricades. They were a power engineer from Brovary Antonina DVORYANETS (aged 61) and a 53-year-old Self-Defense fighter Zurab HURTSIYA from Kirovograd.

They both died of acute heart failure. Obviously, it was caused by the pressure and stress

Another protester, a man from Zhytomyr, Yakiv ZAYKO (aged 73), a deputy of the I-st convocation of Verkhovna Rada, had a heart attack. According to investigators, it was not on the Instytutska street as it was stated in most sources. A former member of parliament died near the Teatralna metro stations, where there were no clashes. No signs of violent death or beating were found on his body.

The siege tightens. The Police is bracing up for the storm

However, taking this barricade still did not grant them open access to the Maidan.

For the first time during the three months of the protest the regime forces burst directly onto Maidan.

The demonstrators manage to impede the attack, building fires from the materials available at hand along the new defense line – from the Independence monument to the barricade near the House of Trade Unions.

There will appear “eyewitnesses” who saw the “Berkut” and “Titushki” to sadistically decapitate the protesters alive. There will be talks about dozens of people tortured to death and missing.

The investigation turns down these rumors. The person on the stretcher was identified – it is a Maidan protester from Western Ukraine. He was not dead, albeit with a bandaged head. He will survive those terrible days and return home.

Opposition MPs release from the Antimaidan camp the participants of the “Peaceful Advance” captured after the battle in Mariyinskyi Park. There were 40 injured demonstration participants, “among them 23 were severely injured”. Antimaidan medical workers provided first aid to all of them.

And then they get under targeted gunfire.

FIRST VICTIMS AMONG LAW ENFORCEMENT STAFF
